Age Limit and Possession Limit

Under Ontario's cannabis laws of 2025, any individual 19 years of age or older is legally allowed to purchase, possess, consume, and cultivate cannabis. Yet, it's critical to remember that this is limited to personal use only. Ontario residents are allowed to possess up to 30 grams of dried cannabis in public, which is aligned with federal law.

Sales Policies and Consumption Locations

Ontario has also reformed its approach to the sales of cannabis, maintaining a hybrid model of private and government-operated sales outlets. The Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario (AGCO) oversees the licensing and regulation of privately-run cannabis stores. Meanwhile, the Ontario Cannabis Store (OCS) serves as the government-controlled online store, responsible for providing a safe, legal channel for cannabis purchase.

The consumption of cannabis in Ontario in 2025 is permitted in private residences, outdoor public spaces (excluding those within 20 meters of playgrounds and school grounds), designated guest rooms in hotels, motels, and inns, residential boats and vehicles following specific guidelines.

Cannabis Cultivation

Key to understanding Ontario's latest cannabis laws is acknowledging the right to grow cannabis for personal use. Ontario residents are allowed to grow up to four plants per residence, not per person, following federal guidelines. This is a game-changing facet of cannabis policy, enabling users to have more control over the cannabis they consume.

Rules for Medical Marijuana

Medicinal cannabis continues to be a prevalent factor in the Ontario cannabis scene. Patients registered with Health Canada's medical marijuana program can purchase cannabis through the Ontario Cannabis Store or grow their own, within established regulations. Furthermore, despite the recreational allowances, different rules apply to medical marijuana users, especially concerning possession and consumption.
